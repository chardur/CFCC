What will I learn with a Simulation and Gaming Degree?

Answer: Students learn foundational principles, theories, and techniques, including game design, simulation development, graphics, multimedia, UX/UI design, and simulation/gaming technologies. Through hands-on projects and case studies, students explore real-world applications across industries. Emphasis is placed on project management, collaboration, and culminates in a capstone project or internship, equipping students with the skills needed for careers in gaming, simulation, education, and beyond.

Career Opportunities:

Game Tester/QA Tester

Play an essential role in ensuring the quality and functionality of video games by identifying bugs, glitches, and other issues. They test games across different platforms and provide feedback to developers.

Junior Game Developer

Assist in the creation and implementation of game features, mechanics, and systems. They may work on programming tasks, asset integration, or level design under the supervision of senior developers.

Game Artist/3D Artist

Create visual elements for games, including characters, environments, props, and special effects. They use 3D modeling, texturing, and animation techniques to bring game worlds to life.

Educational Game Developer

Create games and simulations designed to teach specific concepts or skills in educational settings. They may work for educational institutions, government agencies, or educational software companies.

Simulation Technician

Assist in the setup, operation, and maintenance of simulation equipment and software used in various industries such as healthcare, military training, aviation, and manufacturing.

Augmented Reality (AR) Developer

Design and develop applications that overlay digital information or virtual objects onto the real world using AR technologies. They may work on AR games, marketing applications, or industrial training tools.

Virtual Reality (VR) Developer

Pursue opportunities in creating VR experiences for entertainment, training, education, or other applications. They may work as VR content creators, VR experience designers, or VR developers.

Indie Developer

Some graduates may choose to work independently or in small teams to develop and publish their own games or simulations, either as indie developers or freelancers.
